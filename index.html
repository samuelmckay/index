Clockwork

I don't need a wrist clock
Need not watch the violence

I can be my own tick tock
Akin to birthday virus

Peripheral is sidetracked on their side
Present there, yet broken and seeping

Common phrase, "Look on the brightside"
Perishing and eternal keeping

Can't say we dream of each other
Or that we are the same

Though I know we think like one another
Forever enduring the name

They have thier own virus
Lack of sleep and "on quive"

Draining is too timeless
And reality has too much sieve.

  2. Yellowjackeys

    3. By Yuesef Komunyakaa

    When the plowblade struck
    An old stump hiding under
    The soil like a beggar's
    Rotten tooth, they swarmed up
    & Mister Jackson left the plow
    Wedged like a whaler's harpoon.
    The horse was midnight
    Against the dusk, tethered to somebody's
    Pocketwatch. He shivered, but not
    The way women shook their heads
    Before mirrors at the five
    & dine--a deeper connection
    To the low field's evening star.
    He stood there, in tracechains,
    Lathered in froth, just
    Stopped by a great, goofy
    Calmness. He whinnied
    Once, & then the whole
    Beautiful, blue-black sky
    Fell on his back.
