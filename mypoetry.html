My Poetry

Clockwork

I don't need a wrist clock
Need not watch the violence

I can be my own tick tock
Akin to birthday virus

Peripheral is sidetracked on their side
Present there, yet broken and seeping

Common phase "Look on the bright side"
Perishing and eternal keeping

Can't say that we dream on each other
Or that we are the same

Though I know we think like on another
Forever enduring the name

They have their own virus
Lack of sleep and "On quive"

Draining is too timelese
And reality has too much sieve.

Untitled #1

The Man was struggling with the rabbit
Goddamnit, we could walk on the moon-
pick at craterous acne-
but we can't tune to Wheel of Fortune?
Scrambling our perception of miracles
We could probably do without the satellite signals

lemon garden

that lemon garden fool,
who sees the flower's bloom;
and with such expansive piety, too.
he watched as they rise and fall,
and thinks zilch of it all.
but once the flower begins the crawl
to his calloused green hands,
that fool begins to understand
the flower's blooming despite commands
from the gardener and the flourist
with desires of the purest
into the ugly hands of "your dearest."
that lemon garden fool—
the one used as a tool
to stop the wilt and bloom—
allowed one flower to stay
to bloom as it may
hidden from the stains of doom.

Untitled #2

Days in the sun —
time runs effortlessly,
seen only by lightning;
(might be) gone by now.

Somehow the season
passes by like a train
on track, gone by fast;
derailed at the last moment.
puffs of smoke —
clouds —

Cover the sun.

Found Poem #1 — from Stephen King's It

His mother exclaimed, more than once:

"You could build a clock
that really told time."

"Lonely?" he might have asked in return,

Honestly foozled.

A child blind from birth

Doesn't know he's blind
until someone tells him.

But, lonliness both encompassed his life,

And overreached it:
It simply was.

A sweet dream; the candy was

A sweet reality.

Most of all, to shoot them into his mouth

One by one, like a kid
committing suicide by sugar.

It was really two buildings,

The old stone structure in front.
